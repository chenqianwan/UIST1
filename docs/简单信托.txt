The Trustees are under no duty to make further enquiry in respect of a written consent if a person is signing in the capacity as a legal representative of a Beneficiary.
2.Declaration of Trust of Original and Additional Property
The Trustees shall hold the Trust Fund on the trusts and upon, with and subject to the trusts, powers and provisions contained in this Deed.
The Trustees shall be at liberty at any time or times during the Trust Period to accept or disclaim as an addition to the Trust Fund any money, investments, or other property (including property of an onerous nature), as may be paid or transferred to them or otherwise placed under their control, in any manner, of whatsoever nature and wheresoever situate, from any person, or by will, or by the provisions of any other trust, to be held upon such trusts.
3.Trusts of Income and Capital
The provisions of this Clause shall apply during the Trust Period until, subject to and in default of, any appointment under Clause 4.
The Trustees shall [, with the prior or simultaneous written consent of the Protective Committee,] pay or apply the whole or any part of the Trust Fund and the income thereof to or for the benefit of all or any one or more exclusive of the other or others of the Beneficiaries, at such ages or times, in such shares, if more than one, and in such manner generally as the Trustees shall in their discretion from time to time think fit.
Notwithstanding the provisions of sub-Clause 3(a), the Trustees may at any time during the Trust Period in their discretion accumulate the whole or any part or parts of the income of the Trust Fund as the Trustees may direct and add the accumulations to the capital, subject to sub-Clause 3(c).
The Trustees may apply the whole or any part of the income accumulated under sub-Clause 3(b) as if it were income arising in the then current year.
4.Powers of Appointment and Advancement
The Trustees shall hold the capital and income of the Trust Fund upon trust to or for the benefit of all or any one or more exclusive of the other or others of the Beneficiaries, at such ages or times, in such shares, if more than one, upon such trusts (which may include discretionary or protective powers or trusts) and in such manner generally as [the Protective Committee shall in their discretion and by a declaration in writing appoint, provided that the original of the declaration in writing by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within [fourteen] days of the date of the said declaration].
Notwithstanding the trusts, powers and provisions contained in Clause 3, the Protective Committee shall have power, by a declaration in writing, to instruct the Trustees to appoint that the whole or any part of the Trust Fund and its income shall be held on new trusts in favour of any one or more of the Beneficiaries in such shares and in such manner generally as the Protective Committee thinks fit, provided that the original of the declaration in writing by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said declaration.
An appointment may create any provisions and in particular may create discretionary trusts or dispositive or administrative powers, all exercisable by the Trustees or by any other person.
No exercise of the power conferred by sub-Clauses 4.1 or 4.2 shall invalidate any prior payment or application of all or any part of the capital or income of the Trust Fund under the trusts of this Deed or made under any other power conferred by this Deed or by law.
The exercise of the power of appointment conferred by sub-Clauses 4.1 or 4.2 shall be made by deed (revocable or irrevocable) and shall comply with the provisions of sub-Clause 11.4.
The Protective Committee may, by a declaration in writing, appoint the whole or any part of the capital of the Trust Fund by paying or transferring the same to the trustees of any other trust or settlement for the benefit of any of the Beneficiaries, whether or not the proper law of such other trust or settlement shall be the Proper Law of this Trust, provided that the original of the declaration in writing by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said declaration.
The exercise of the powers conferred by sub-Clause 4.6 shall comply with the provisions of sub-Clause 11.4 and be subject to the following provisions:
upon the payment or transfer of any money or other property to the trustees of any such trust or settlement, the Trustees shall not be bound to see to the further application of such money or property;  and 
the Trustees may make such payment or transfer to the trustees of a discretionary trust, notwithstanding that the Beneficiary for whose benefit the power is exercised is only a discretionary object of such trust.
The Protective Committee may, by a declaration in writing, instruct the Trustee to pay or apply the whole or any part of the Trust Fund to or for the maintenance, advancement or benefit of any Beneficiary.  The statutory powers of maintenance and advancement are excluded.
5.Final Trusts
Upon the expiration of the Trust Period, and subject to any appointment made under Clause 4, if and so far as not wholly disposed of for any reason whatever by the above provisions, the capital and income of the Trust Fund shall be held upon trust for such charity or charities as the Protective Committee shall, by a declaration in writing, in their absolute discretion select and in such shares as the Protective Committee shall determine, provided that the original of the declaration in writing by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said declaration, and the receipt in writing of the treasurer or other officer of such charity or charities shall be a good discharge to the Protective Committee and the Trustees.
6.Protection of the Trustees in Respect of Distributions
The Trustees may distribute the Trust Fund without having ascertained that there is no Beneficiary whose parents were not married to each other at the time of his birth (or who claims through a person whose parents were not so married) and the Trustees shall not be liable to any Beneficiary of whose existence they had no actual notice at the time of distribution.
7.Payments to Charity
In exercise of the trusts and powers hereinbefore contained, the Trustees may [at the request of the Protective Committee,] pay or apply any part of the capital or income of the Trust Fund to or for the benefit of any charity [approved by the Protective Committee] and any such payment or application shall be deemed to be for the benefit of such Beneficiary and the Trustees shall not be restricted by any rule of law as to the amount or manner of such payment or application and the receipt in writing of the treasurer or other officer of any charity shall be a good discharge to the Trustees for any capital or income paid to such charity.
8.Power to Exclude Beneficiaries and Disclaimer
The Protective Committee may by a declaration in writing made at any time or times during the Trust Period declare that the person or persons or members of a class named or specified (whether or not ascertained) in such declaration who are, would or might but for this Clause be or become a Beneficiary or Beneficiaries or be otherwise able to benefit hereunder as the case may be:
shall be wholly or partially excluded from future benefit hereunder;
shall cease to be a Beneficiary or Beneficiaries,
and any such declaration may be irrevocable or revocable during the Trust Period and shall have effect from the date specified in the said declaration provided that this power shall not be capable of being exercised so as to derogate from any interest to which any Beneficiary has previously become indefeasibly entitled whether in possession or in reversion or otherwise, and provided that the original of the declaration in writing by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said deed.
Any person of full age to whom or for whose benefit any capital or interest of the Trust Fund may be liable whether directly or indirectly to be appointed transferred or applied in any manner whatsoever by or in consequence of an exercise of any trust power or discretion vested in the Trustees or in any other person may by a declaration in writing received by the Trustees during the Trust Period either revocably (but revocable during the Trust Period only) or irrevocably:
disclaim his, her or its interest as an object of any trust power or discretion either wholly or with respect to any specified part or share of such capital or income;
cease to be a Beneficiary,
and such declaration shall have effect from the date that the same is received by the Trustees.
9.Power to add Beneficiaries
The Protective Committee shall have power at any time or times during the Trust Period by a declaration in writing to add to the class of Beneficiaries such person or class of persons as the Protective Committee shall in their absolute discretion determine, provided that the original of the declaration in writing by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said deed.
Any such addition shall be made by deed, revocable or irrevocable, and shall take effect from the date specified in the deed (not being earlier than the date of such deed) and shall comply with sub-Clause 11.4 herein, provided that the original of the deed by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said deed.
10.Additional Powers of the Trustees
The Trustees shall have the powers, discretions, rights and immunities set out in Schedule 2.  [All powers of investment are subject to the prior or simultaneous written consent of the Protective Committee.]
11.Exercise of Powers
The Trustees shall exercise the powers and discretions vested in them as they shall think most expedient for the benefit of all or any of the Beneficiaries under this Trust and may exercise (or refrain from exercising) any power or discretion for the benefit of any one or more of them without being obliged to consider the interest of the others or other.
Subject to the previous sub-Clause 11.1 [and subject to the prior or simultaneous written consent of the Protective Committee,] where hereby required, every discretion vested in the Trustees shall be absolute and exercisable at their absolute and uncontrolled discretion and the Trustees shall have the same discretion in deciding whether or not to exercise any such power.
The Trustees shall have power to determine all questions and matters of doubt which may arise in the course of the management, administration, realisation, liquidation, partition, or winding up, of the Trust Fund[, provided that for all questions and matters of doubt which may rise in the course of the liquidation, partition or winding up, the Trustees shall enter into discussion with the Protective Committee before making any determination in respect of such questions and matters].
Despite anything contained elsewhere in this Trust, no discretion or power conferred by this Trust on the Trustees or on any other person shall be exercisable after the expiration of the Trust Period or in such a way as to infringe any rule against perpetuities or excessive accumulations applicable hereto.
If there shall be more than one Trustee, the Trustees shall act, and shall make all decisions relating to the Trust by majority vote.  The majority of the Trustees my, by written instrument or otherwise, make any decision and take any action relating to the Trust, after advising the remaining Trustee or Trustees, PROVIDED THAT failure so to advise shall not invalidate any action or majority decision.  If there shall be an equal number of Trustees for or against any proposed action or decision, then such action shall be deemed not to be adopted.  Any Trustee who was opposed to or did not note in favour of such action or decision shall not be liable in relation to same, except for his/her/its own actual fraud or wilful default. 
The provisions of sub-Clauses 11.1 and 11.2 hereof shall apply mutatis mutandis to the Protective Committee.
12.Restriction and Release of Powers
The Trustees shall have power by instrument in writing revocable during the Trust Period or irrevocable to release or to any extent restrict the future exercise of any powers hereby or by law conferred on them notwithstanding the fiduciary nature of any such powers.
13.Power of Appointment of New or Additional Trustees
The number of Trustees shall not at any time exceed four nor be less than one regardless of whether or not the sole Trustee is an individual or a Company. 
If any Trustee whether original, additional, or substituted, shall die or being a Company shall be dissolved, or shall give notice of his or its desire to withdraw and be discharged from the trusts hereof under sub-Clause 13.4, or shall refuse or become unfit to act, or shall be removed under sub-Clause 13.5, then the Protective Committee may by deed appoint one or more other individuals or Companies (whether resident within or without the Cayman Islands) to be a Trustee of this Trust in place of the Trustee so deceased, removed, dissolved, desiring to withdraw, or be discharged, or refusing or becoming unfit, to act.
To the extent permissible the stipulation contained in Section 6(c) of the Act that a trustee shall not be discharged from his trust unless there will be either a trust corporation or at least two individuals to act as trustees to perform the trust is hereby excluded.
If any Trustee shall at any time be desirous of withdrawing and being discharged from the trusts of this Trust the Trustee may do so by giving 1 month notice in writing to the person having for the time being power to appoint new or additional Trustees of this Trust.  Such notice shall operate as an effectual relinquishment of the trusts and the Trustee giving the same shall thereupon cease to be a Trustee to all intents and purposes except as to the acts and deeds necessary for the appointment of a new Trustee or new Trustees in his or its place and for the proper vesting of the Trust Fund in the continuing Trustee or Trustees or otherwise as the case may require which shall be executed or done at the expense of the Trust Fund. 
The person for the time being having power to appoint new Trustees shall have power by 1 month notice in writing to remove any Trustee or Trustees and thereupon such Trustee or Trustees shall cease to be a Trustee or Trustees to all intents and purposes except as to the acts and deeds necessary for the proper vesting of the Trust Fund in the continuing or new Trustee or Trustees or otherwise as the case may require.
The office of a Trustee shall be ipso facto determined and vacated if such Trustee being an individual shall be found to have lost his mental capacity or is Disabled or if he shall become bankrupt.
Acts and deeds done or executed for the proper vesting of the Trust Fund in new or additional Trustees shall be done and executed by the continuing or retiring Trustee or Trustees at the expense of the income or capital of the Trust Fund provided that an outgoing Trustee who is liable as a Trustee of this Trust or who may at the death of any person be liable as a former Trustee of this Trust for any taxes wherever they may be imposed and of whatsoever nature shall not be bound to transfer the Trust Fund unless reasonable security is provided for indemnifying such outgoing Trustee against such liability.
Any new or additional Trustee appointed under this Clause or by a court of competent jurisdiction shall have such power rights and benefits as to remuneration or otherwise as at or prior to his appointment may be agreed in writing (in case of a Trustee appointed as hereinbefore provided) between such new or additional Trustee and the person making such appointment or (in the case of a Trustee appointed by a court) as the order appointing such Trustee may direct.
14.Indemnity of Retired or Removed Trustees
If a Trustee retires or is removed or becomes by reason of residence or place of incorporation incapable of acting as a Trustee, such Trustee its officers, directors and employees, shall be released from and indemnified against all claims, demands, actions, proceedings, and accounts of any kind, on the part of any person (whether in existence or not) actually or prospectively interested under this Trust for or in respect of the Trust Fund or the income of the Trust Fund or thing done or omitted in execution or purported execution of such trust other than and except only actions:
arising from any actual fraud, fraud, breach of trust, negligence, gross negligence, wilful default or other default to which such Trustee or (in the case of a corporate Trustee) any of its officers, directors and/or employees were party or privy;  and 
to recover from such Trustee the Trust Fund or any part thereof in the possession of such Trustee or previously received by such Trustee (or in the case of a corporate Trustee) any of its officers, directors and/or employees and converted to their use.
15.Trustees Indemnity
In the execution of the trusts and powers of this Trust no Trustee, its officers, directors, employees’ and/or affiliates shall be liable for any loss to the Trust Fund arising in consequence of the failure, depreciation, or loss of any investments, made in good faith, or by reason of any mistake, or omission made in good faith, or of any other matter or thing, except arising from actual fraud, fraud, breach of trust,  negligence, gross negligence, wilful default or other default on the part of the Trustee, its officers, directors, employees and/or affiliates who is sought to be made liable. 
16.Remuneration OF TRUSTEES and PROTECTIVE COMMITTEE MEMBERS
Any Trustee or Protective Committee Member who is a person engaged in any profession or business or any such person associated with such Trustee or Protective Committee Member associated or beneficially interested or in any way connected with such Trustee or Protective Committee Member shall be entitled to charge and be paid all usual professional or other charges for business done and time spent and services rendered by him or his firm in the execution of the trusts and powers of this Trust whether in the ordinary course of his profession or business or not and although not of a nature requiring the employment of a professional person and any such person shall be entitled to retain any commission which would or may become payable as a direct or indirect result of any dealing with property which is or may become subject to this Trust.
Any Trustee for the time being who shall be a Company authorised to undertake trust business shall be entitled in addition to reimbursement of its proper expenses to remuneration for its services in accordance with such Company's published terms and conditions for trust business in force from time to time.
No Trustee, Protective Committee Member, or director or other officer of any Company which is a Trustee or Protective Committee Member hereof shall be liable to account for any remuneration or other profit received by him in consequence of his acting as or being appointed to be a director or other officer or servant of any Company even though his appointment was procured by an exercise by him or by the Trustees of voting rights attached to securities in the Trust Fund or by any abstention from exercising such voting rights.
Any Trustee or any associate of a Trustee who carries on the business of banking may act as banker for this Trust on the same terms as those made with an ordinary customer without being liable to account to the Trust for any profits earned thereby.
17.The ProtectiVE COMMITTEE
The first member of the Protective Committee is the Original Protector, who has accepted office by executing this Deed.
The appointment of a Protective Committee Member is subject to such Protective Committee Member’s acceptance by notice in writing to the Trustees.
[The Original Protector or (if the Original Protector is dead, Incapacitated or otherwise unable or unwilling to act),] the Protective Committee may by written instrument:
(a)	irrevocably appoint any person to be an additional Protective Committee Member and, if the Trustees have been given written notice of the appointment and if the person appointed consents in writing, that person shall immediately become a Protective Committee Member; and
(b)	nominate a person to become a Protective Committee Member on any vacancy arising or on a specified person ceasing to be a Protective Committee Member.  If that nomination remains unrevoked, the Trustees have been given written notice of the nomination and the person nominated consents in writing, that person shall immediately become a Protective Committee Member when a vacancy arises or when the specified member ceases to be a Protective Committee Member. 
Notwithstanding the foregoing provisions of this Clause, there shall not be more than five (5) members of the Protective Committee.
[The Original Protector may by written instrument remove a Protective Committee Member if notice is given to the Trustees.]
A Protective Committee Member may resign at any time for such reasons as may appear to such Protective Committee Member to be sufficient and without any necessity for obtaining authorisation from the court by a written notice (PC Member Resignation Notice) delivered by such Protective Committee Member to the other Protective Committee Members and the Trustees. After the expiration of [thirty (30)] days from the date of the PC Member Resignation Notice, or such shorter period as may be agreed in writing between such resigning Protective Committee Member and the Trustees, such resigning Protective Committee Member shall thereafter cease to be a Protective Committee Member, provided that, where such resigning Protective Committee Member is the sole Protective Committee Member, the resignation of such resigning Protective Committee Member shall not be effective unless and until such resigning Protective Committee Member shall have appointed another person to be a Protective Committee Member in his/her/its place.
The Protective Committee shall act by a [majority of at least three-fourth in number] of the Protective Committee Members. If the number of the Protective Committee Members reduced to two, such Protective Committee Members shall act unanimously. If the number of the Protective Committee Members reduced to one, such Protective Committee Member shall act alone.
A person ceases to be a Protective Committee Member in the event of:
if the Protective Committee Members is an individual, his or her death, or him or her suffering Disability, or him or her becoming unsound of mind, or his or her refusal, unfitness or being incapable of acting as a Protective Committee Member [(including, without limitation, not replying to requests made by the Trustees or other remaining Protective Committee Member to such Protective Committee Member from time to time for more than ninety (90) days)]; or
if the Protective Committee Member is a company, on dissolution; 
in either case, his, her or its resignation by written notice to the Trustees.
If at any time there is no Protective Committee Member or no Protective Committee Member able and willing to act, [all the Beneficiaries][ If the Beneficiaries are a class instead of persons named, then it would not be possible to obtain consent by all the Beneficiaries. ] may appoint a Protective Committee Member.
A Protective Committee Member shall not be appointed as a trustee.  A Trustee shall not be appointed as a Protective Committee Member.
A Protective Committee Member shall not be liable for a loss to the Trust Fund unless that loss was caused by his or her own actual fraud, wilful default or gross negligence.
A Protective Committee Member shall be entitled to be indemnified from the Trust Fund during and after their being a Protective Committee Member of this Trust respectively for any liabilities incurred in his or her capacity as such, unless they were caused by his or her own actual fraud, wilful default or gross negligence.
The Protective Committee shall have the powers and discharge the duties respectively conferred or imposed upon him by the provisions of this Deed.
Each Protective Committee Member who is an individual shall have the power, subject to the  consent of the Protective Committee acting by a [majority of at least three-fourth in number] of the Protective Committee Members, to appoint by will or codicil or by deed, revocable or irrevocable, any person who is not a Trustee or such a director, officer or employee as aforesaid to succeed him as a Protective Committee Member in the event of his/her death or in the event that he/she suffers from Disability, becomes of unsound mind or is otherwise unable to carry out his duties as a Protective Committee Member and, in the instrument effecting such appointment, such Protective Committee Member may also appoint another eligible person or, in order of priority, other eligible persons to act as a Protective Committee Member in the event that the person so appointed to succeed him/her is dead, suffers from Disability or of unsound mind or unwilling or unable to carry out the duties of a Protective Committee Member.
18.Powers of THE ProtectIVE COMMITTEE 
The powers of the Protective Committee Members are personal in nature. 
The Protective Committee is under no duty to enquire into or interfere with the management or conduct of this Trust, unless they have actual knowledge of circumstances which call for enquiry.
The Protective Committee shall consider the appropriateness of any act before exercising their powers.
The Protective Committee has the same rights as a beneficiary to receive information concerning this Trust and its administration from the Trustees and to inspect and take copies of trust documents.
19.[Release or Abeyance Of The Powers of THE PROTECTIVE COMMITTTEE 
The Protective Committee may from time to time by written notice to the Trustees (a memorandum of which shall be endorsed on or permanently attached to this Deed) declare (either generally or in relation to any particular act or acts and either permanently or for such period as shall be specified in the notice) that any act or acts herein declared to require the consent or approval of the Protective Committee shall not require such consent or approval and the notice shall be effective according to its terms.] 
20.Liability of ProtectIVE COMMITTEE
The Protective Committee shall not be liable for a loss to the Trust Fund unless that loss was caused by his own actual fraud. 
The Protective Committee is entitled to be indemnified from the Trust Fund for all liabilities incurred in his capacity as such unless he was caused by his own actual fraud, fraud, gross negligence, wilful default or other default. 
21.Proper Law and Forum for Administration
This Trust is established under the laws of the Cayman Islands and, subject to the following provisions, the construction and effect of this Trust shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Cayman Islands, the courts of which shall be the forum for the administration of this Trust.
The Trustees shall have power, subject to the application (if any) of the rule against perpetuities, to carry on the general administration of these trusts in any jurisdiction in the world. This power shall be exercisable whether or not the law of such jurisdiction is for the time being the Proper Law of the Trust or the courts of such jurisdiction are for the time being the forum for the administration of these trusts, and whether or not the Trustees or any of them are resident, incorporated or domiciled in, or otherwise connected with, such jurisdiction.
The Trustees may [, with the prior or simultaneous consent or approval of the Protective Committee,] at any time declare in writing that, from the date of such declaration or from a later date specified, the Proper Law of the Trust shall be that of any specified jurisdiction. No exercise of this power shall be effective unless the law of the jurisdiction specified is one under which all, or substantially all, of the trusts, powers and provisions contained in this Deed remain enforceable and capable of being exercised and so taking effect.
On or following any exercise of the power contained in sub-Clause 21.3, the Trustees shall, by deed [and with the prior or simultaneous consent or approval of the Protective Committee], make such consequential alterations or additions to this Trust as they consider necessary or desirable to ensure that, so far as may be possible, the trusts, powers and provisions of this Trust shall be as valid and effective as they were immediately prior to such change. 
The Trustees may [, with the prior or simultaneous consent or approval of the Protective Committee,] at any time, declare in writing that, from the date of such declaration or from a later date specified, the forum for the administration of these trusts shall be the courts of any specified jurisdiction. 
22.Power of PROTECTiVE COMMITTEE to Amend
The Protective Committee shall have the power at any time and form time to time, by an instrument in writing or deed supplemental hereto, to amend, add, or delete any or all of the provisions of this Deed, as well as any powers or provisions of an administrative, dispositive or management character as the Protective Committee may consider to be expedient for the benefit of the Trust, provided that the original of such instrument in writing or deed supplemental hereto by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said instrument in writing or deed supplemental hereto. 
[If at any time there is no Protective Committee Member, all the Beneficiaries (or the Beneficiary, if there is one Beneficiary only) shall, have the power by deed to amend, add or delete any powers or provisions of an administrative, dispositive or management character as the Trustees may consider to be expedient for the benefit of the Trust, provided that the original of such instrument in writing by which this power is exercised shall be provided to the Trustees within fourteen days of the date of the said instrument in writing.]

This Trust shall be irrevocable.
